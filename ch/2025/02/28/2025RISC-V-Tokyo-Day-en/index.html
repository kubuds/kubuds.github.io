Kubuds Tech Debuts at 2025 RISC-V Tokyo Day

2025-02-28 Kubuds

On February 27, 2025 RISC-V Tokyo Day was successfully held in Tokyo, Japan. As a new member of the RISC-V open-source ecosystem, 【KUBUDS TECH】 made its appearance as a Top Sponsor. Wu Wei, Manager of the RISC-V Prosperity 2036 and Co-founder of Kubuds Tech, delivered a keynote speech, comprehensively showcasing the growth journey of Kubuds Technology. At the same time, a number of cutting-edge technical achievements were displayed at the on-site booth.

RISC-V Prosperity 2036: Mechanism, Progress, and Vision

As the Manager of the RISC-V Prosperity 2036, Wu Wei detailed its operational mechanisms and future plans:

  • Operational Mechanism: No board of directors, no membership fees, relying entirely on community self-governance. Any enterprise or developer can freely join or leave, promoting projects through GitHub collaboration.
  • Core Progress: Over 40 manufacturers and 500+ developers have joined. Projects such as the “Development Board Drift Plan” and “Open Source Intern Joint Training” have been implemented, cumulatively porting and optimizing over 200 software titles to the RISC-V platform.
  • Target Vision: By 2036, promote the adaptation of 1000 key commercial software titles for RISC-V, covering core areas such as AI, edge computing, and healthcare, to end the “first-mover disadvantage.”

The Birth of Kubuds Tech: Originating from the RISC-V Dreams of a Group of Young People

As an active participant in the RISC-V Prosperity 2036, Kubuds Technology has been committed to the construction of the basic software ecosystem such as compilers, simulators, and even operating systems. With the mission of “providing commercial-grade technical support for RISC-V”, it has quickly become an ecological software partner of many high-performance RISC-V chips and manufacturers. The manager introduced, “The growth of Kubuds proves that the open-source spirit and commercial value can coexist. We are both technical contributors and ecological beneficiaries.”

Booth Highlights

At the booth of Kubuds Technology this time, a number of innovative projects and technical solutions were exhibited. The RISC-V Prosperity 2036 showcased its technical landscape overseas in a panoramic manner for the first time.

Cutting-edge Tech Demo

Live demonstration of migrating x86 applications to the RISC-V platform within 5 minutes.

Integrated LLVM compiler, Gem5 simulator, and V8 runtime, supporting one-click deployment for edge AI models.

Full-Stack Software Service Matrix

  • Emulator Custom Development: Based on open-source tools like Spike, QEMU, GEM5, providing verification services from instruction set simulation to full-system simulation, accelerating RISC-V chip hardware development, and supporting SystemC TLM2 standard models.
  • RISC-V System Adaptation: Covering various operating systems like OpenHarmony, openEuler, RT-Thread, achieving deep optimization from RTOS to Linux, helping customers rapidly deploy various customized and industrial-grade embedded solutions. Toolchain optimization: Based on the GCC/LLVM cross-architecture (x86_64/ARM/RISC-V) adaptation service, it integrates automatic vectorization and AI acceleration libraries (MLIR/TVM), with significantly improved performance.
  • Embedded Chip One-Stop Solution: Providing bare-metal system porting, driver development, dedicated acceleration library optimization, and platform performance evaluation for ARM/RISC-V, covering multiple fields including robotics and industrial scenarios.

Showcase of Selected RISC-V Prosperity 2036 Achievements

  • Commercial Software Porting: Collaborating with 100+ chip manufacturers and 500+ ISVs, completed RISC-V adaptation for 200+ key software titles, covering AI, edge computing, industrial simulation, and other fields.
  • Developer Ecosystem Innovation: Showcased results from the Tokyo node of the “Development Board Drift Plan,” having distributed over 80 devices, with donation points from manufacturers like Xuantie and Milk-V exceeding 260,000.
  • Talent Reserve Initiative: The Open Source Intern project has trained 350 university students, involving them in 40+ projects, including core component development for the Linux kernel and V8 engine.

Acknowledgments and Outlook

Kubuds Tech extends sincere gratitude to RISC-V Ambassador Fu Wei, the Tokyo organizers, PLCT Lab, Milk-V, and Sipeed.

Participation in this Tokyo Day marks a crucial step in Kubuds Tech’s global RISC-V strategy. With the continuous advancement of the RISC-V Prosperity 2036, Chinese open-source power is injecting more possibilities into the RISC-V ecosystem.
